Project Overview

The Digital Literacy Portfolio is an initiative developed as part of my role as a Student Digital Ambassador. This project aims to promote awareness, responsibility, and effective use of digital tools among students, helping them navigate the online world safely and professionally.

In today’s technology-driven environment, digital literacy is an essential skill. This portfolio serves as a comprehensive resource that covers key aspects such as creating a professional online presence, practicing safe internet habits, understanding cyber threats, and using digital tools efficiently for academic and personal growth.

The project includes informative content, practical examples, and awareness materials such as case studies, quizzes, and visual guides. It is designed to educate peers on how to protect their personal information, communicate respectfully online, and build a positive digital footprint.

By sharing this portfolio with my batch and presenting it to the faculty committee, the goal is to encourage responsible digital behavior and empower students to become informed and confident digital citizens.

Module 1

As a Student Digital Ambassador, this task involves creating a one-page infographic using Canva to help students understand digital literacy and its importance. The visual should clearly cover three main areas: the meaning of digital literacy, useful digital tools for students, and safe internet practices.

Module 2

This task focuses on building a professional digital presence by creating profiles on key platforms like LinkedIn, Kaggle, and Stack Overflow. Students must complete their LinkedIn profile by adding education details such as degree, institution, and expected graduation year.

Module 3

This task helps students explore coding practice and collaboration tools.

In Part A, students must create an account on HackerRank and complete a beginner-level challenge.

In Part B, students use Google Workspace to create a Google Form with exactly five questions for a Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz, including at least one multiple-choice and one short-answer question. They must also link the responses to Google Sheets, share the form link in the README, and upload screenshots of both the form and response sheet.

Module 4

This task focuses on developing professional communication skills and promoting responsible online behavior.

In Part A, students must write two formal emails—one to a professor requesting an assignment extension and another to an internship coordinator expressing interest in a summer internship. Both emails should follow proper email etiquette, including a clear subject line, professional greeting, structured body, and appropriate sign-off.

In Part B, students must create a checklist with at least five Do’s and five Don’ts for responsible social media use.

Module 5

This task focuses on building awareness about cybercrime and promoting safe online practices.

In Part A, students must research a cybercrime such as phishing and write case study explaining what the crime is, how it happens step-by-step, who is targeted, and its consequences.

In Part B, students must create a “Stay Safe Online” checklist with at least eight actionable tips relevant to college students in India, including at least two tips on UPI or financial safety. They must also include reporting options such as the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) and Helpline 1930.
