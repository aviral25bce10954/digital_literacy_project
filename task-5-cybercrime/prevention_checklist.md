* #### &#x20;                              Essential Prevention Tips for Digital Safety
* Never Scan a QR Code to Receive Money: Always remember that a QR code scan is only required to make a payment, never to receive one.

  ---
* UPI PIN is Only for Sending: You never need to enter your UPI PIN or scan your fingerprint to receive money into your bank account.

  ---
* Verify the Sender's Identity: If you receive a call from someone claiming to be a bank official, hang up and call the official customer care number mentioned on the bank’s website or your physical debit card.

  ---
* Avoid Remote Access Apps: Never download apps like AnyDesk, TeamViewer, or RustDesk on the advice of a stranger. These apps allow hackers to see your screen and steal your passwords in real-time.

  ---
* Check the SMS Header: Official banks use verified headers (e.g., AD-HDFCBK or VK-ICICIB). If an alert comes from a regular 10-digit mobile number, it is 100% a scam.

  ---
* Don't Click on Shortened Links: Be wary of links from services like bit.ly or tinyurl sent via SMS. They often lead to fake "phishing" pages that look exactly like your bank's login portal.

  ---
* Set Transaction Limits: Use your mobile banking app to set a daily limit on UPI transactions. This ensures that even if a breach occurs, the loss is minimized.

  ---
* Use Biometric Locks: Enable fingerprint or face-lock for your payment apps (PhonePe, Google Pay, Paytm) so that no one can open them even if your phone is unlocked.

  ---
* Keep Your OS Updated: Always keep your phone's software and banking apps updated. These updates often contain "Security Patches" that protect you from new types of malware.

  ---
* ###### Report Immediately (1930): If you or someone you know falls victim to a fraud, immediately call the national cybercrime helpline 1930 or   report it at www.cybercrime.gov.in within the first "Golden Hour" (1-2 hours) to freeze the stolen funds.



* ###### National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in): Use this official portal to register a formal complaint for any digital fraud to initiate legal action.



* ###### National Helpline 1930: Call this number immediately after a financial fraud to freeze the stolen funds within the "Golden Hour."
