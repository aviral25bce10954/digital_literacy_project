### Case Study: The "KYC Suspension" UPI \& Banking Fraud

##### 1\. What the Crime is:

This is a sophisticated form of Vishing (Voice Phishing) and UPI Fraud. Scammers impersonate bank officials or service providers to induce fear in the victim, eventually leading to unauthorized access to their bank accounts or UPI wallets.



##### 2\. How it Typically Happens (Step-by-Step):



Step 1: The Panic Trigger: The victim receives an SMS stating, "Your Bank/KYC has expired. Your account will be blocked within 24 hours. Call 9XXXXXXXXX to update."



Step 2: The Professional Impersonation: When the victim calls, the scammer speaks professionally, often using banking terminology to gain trust. They confirm the victim’s name and basic details (often leaked from third-party databases).



Step 3: The Remote Access: The scammer asks the victim to download a "verification app" like AnyDesk or TeamViewer. These are legitimate remote-desktop apps, but in this context, they allow the scammer to see the victim's mobile screen in real-time.



Step 4: The Theft: The scammer asks the victim to perform a small ₹10 "test transaction" via UPI. As the victim types their UPI PIN, the scammer sees it via the screen-sharing app and simultaneously captures the OTP.



Step 5: The Drain: Within minutes, the scammer initiates multiple high-value transfers, emptying the victim’s account before the victim realizes the screen is being controlled.



##### 3\. Who is Usually Targeted:

Elderly citizens, retired professionals, and people who are not tech-savvy are the primary targets. However, even students and young professionals often fall for the "urgency" of the message and bypass basic security protocols.

##### 

##### 4\. Real Incident Source:

A prominent case was reported by The Times of India (2023), where a retired professional in Mumbai lost over ₹7 Lakhs after clicking a suspicious link sent via SMS for "Electricity Bill KYC Update." The scammers used a remote access tool to siphon funds within 15 minutes of the call.



##### 5\. Consequences:



Financial Ruin: Loss of life savings in a matter of minutes.



Data Breach: Permanent exposure of PAN, Aadhaar, and banking credentials.



Legal Hurdles: The difficulty of tracking "mule accounts" (where the money is transferred) makes recovery rates extremely low (less than 10-15%).
